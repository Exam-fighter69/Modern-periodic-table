Radon

Radon is a chemical element with the symbol Rn and atomic number 86. It is a radioactive, colorless, odorless, tasteless noble gas that occurs naturally in the environment. Radon is formed from the decay of radium, which is found in soil, rocks, and minerals. It is the heaviest noble gas and is considered to be one of the densest substances that remains a gas under normal conditions.



Physical properties of Radon are :

  1. Radon is a colourless gas, 7.5 times heavier than air and more than 100 times heavier than hydrogen.
  2. Radon is a chemical element with the symbol Rn and atomic number 86.
  3. It is a noble gas and is the heaviest of the known gases.
  4. radon is a heavy, radioactive, and highly soluble gas with a short half-life.
