Thullium

Thulium is a chemical element with the symbol Tm and atomic number 69. It is the thirteenth and third-last element in the lanthanide series. Like the other lanthanides, the most common oxidation state is +3, seen in its oxide, halides and other compounds; however, the +2 oxidation state can also be stable1. Thulium is found principally in the mineral monazite, which contains about 20 parts per million. It is extracted by ion exchange and solvent extraction. The metal is obtained by reducing the anhydrous fluoride with calcium, or reducing the oxide with lanthanum.



Physical properties of Thullium are :

  1. Thulium is a lanthanide element, it has a bright silvery-gray luster and can be cut by a knife.
  2. It is the least abundant of the rare earths and its metal is easy to work.
  3. Thulium was discovered by Per Teodor Cleve in 1879.
