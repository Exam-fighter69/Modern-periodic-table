Lead

Lead is a chemical element with the symbol Pb (derived from the Latin word plumbum) and atomic number 82. It is a soft, dense, bluish-gray metal that has been used for thousands of years due to its low melting point, corrosion resistance, and malleability.



Physical properties of Lead are :

  1. Lead is a bluish-white lustrous metal.
  2. It is very resistant to corrosion but tarnishes upon exposure to air.
  3. It is very soft, highly malleable, ductile, and a relatively poor conductor of electricity.
  4. Lead isotopes are the end products of each of the three series of naturally occurring radioactive elements.
