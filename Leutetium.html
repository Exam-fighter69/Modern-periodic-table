Lutetium

Lutetium is used in research. Its compounds are used as hosts for scintillators and X-ray phosphors, and the oxide is used in optical lenses. The element behaves as a typical rare earth, forming a series of compounds in oxidation state +3, such as lutetium sesquioxide, sulfate, and chloride.



Physical properties of Lutetium are :

  1. Lutetium is also shiny white but is a solid and a hard metal.
  2. The hardness of the metals in the lanthanide series increases with an increase in atomic number thus Lutetium is a very hard metal.
  3. It reacts slowly with water, but dissolves rapidly in acids.
  4. It exists in trivalent state in compounds.
