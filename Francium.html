Francium

Francium is a chemical element with the symbol Fr and atomic number 87. It is a highly radioactive metal that is very rare in nature. Francium was discovered in 1939 by French chemist Marguerite Perey while working at the Curie Institute in Paris. It was named after France, the country of its discovery.



Physical properties of Francium are :

  1. Francium is a heavy, unstable, radioactive metal with a maximum half-life of only 22 minutes.
  2. It has a low melting point (27 oC, 81 oF) and, if enough of it could be accumulated.
  3. it would be liquid in a warm room.
  4. Francium is the second rarest element in the Earth's crust, next to astatine.
