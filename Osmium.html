Osmium

A shiny, silver metal that resists corrosion. It is the densest of all the elements and is twice as dense as lead. Osmium has only a few uses. It is used to produce very hard alloys for fountain pen tips, instrument pivots, needles and electrical contacts.



Physical properties of Osmium are :

  1. Osmium is a bluish-white and shiny metal.
  2. Osmium has the lowast vapor pressure and the highest melting point among the platinum group of metals.
  3. Osmium's density is slightly more than iridium hence is credited as the heaviest element.
  4. Osmium is very hard and is brittle even at very high temperatures.
