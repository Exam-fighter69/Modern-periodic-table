Radium

Radium is a chemical element with the symbol Ra and atomic number 88. It is a highly radioactive and rare metal that is found in small amounts in the Earth's crust. Radium was discovered by Marie and Pierre Curie in 1898 and was named after the Latin word "radius," meaning ray, due to its intense radioactivity.



Physical properties of Radium are :

  1. Radium is silvery, lustrous, soft, intensely radioactive.
  2. It readily oxidizes on exposure to air, turning from almost pure white to black.
  3. Radium is luminescent, corrodes in water to form radium hydroxide.
  4. Although is the heaviest member of the alkaline-earth group it is the most volatile.
